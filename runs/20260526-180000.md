# Run reflection — buddhist-history-textual-timeline

## What worked well

- **Web research phase was the backbone.** The Hackett/Holder PDF (extracted via pdftotext after WebFetch returned binary), Wikipedia's Pali Canon/Abhidhamma/Khuddaka articles, BuddhistDoor, obo.genaud.net, and encyclopediaofbuddhism.org together provided comprehensive dateable ranges for essentially every text in scope.
- **Abeynayake's two-stratum model** for the Khuddaka Nikāya mapped cleanly to existing scholarship; the table format (text-by-text with early vs. late column) was the right presentation choice.
- **Frauwallner's proto-Vinaya hypothesis** and his dating framework for the Khandhakas (~350 BCE) vs. Parivāra (later) gave the Vinaya section a structural backbone that most sources agree with.
- **Angle triage at Phase 1** correctly identified Archaeology/Inscriptions, History (primary), Cross-school/Āgama, Sociology, and Abhidhamma as APPLICABLE; this kept the research focused on dating evidence rather than doctrinal content.

## What was difficult

- **Calibre unavailable throughout.** Another agent held the Calibre lock for the entire run. Mitigated by direct folder scanning (identifying Frauwallner #4759, Hinüber #6246, Norman #11141, Lamotte #15313, Warder #3283, Gombrich #923 and #5459 from directory listings) and recording them as `library_refs` in the frontmatter. The actual Frauwallner snippets on the relative chronology of the seven Abhidhamma books remain a gap.
- **Buddha's date: "definite vs. indefinite" is not binary.** The short chronology (c. 400 BCE) is majority scholarly consensus, but even within it Gombrich, Bechert, Norman differ by 50+ years. The note addresses this by giving all variants but the lack of epigraphic anchor for the 5th century BCE means even the "scholarly majority" range is INDEFINITE.
- **Mahāyāna sūtra dating.** Most dates are manuscript-terminus-based (Gandhāra finds push Prajñāpāramitā to 1st century BCE, possibly earlier) but original composition vs. redaction vs. stabilized form are three different dateable events for the same text. The note signals this complexity but a full treatment would require Silk, Buswell, or dedicated Mahāyāna scholarship.

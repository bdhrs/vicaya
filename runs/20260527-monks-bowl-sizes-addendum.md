## What surprised me
- The Samantapāsādikā gives *two* equivalences for pattha — ½ nāḷī (bowl rule) and ≈ 1 nāḷī (Verañjā ration) — and they reconcile cleanly via the dry-grain-vs-cooked-rice distinction. Worth keeping in the note as a worked example of "apparent contradiction = different stages of the same process."
- The Samantapāsādikā on Verañjā *itself* quotes the Akittijātaka verse to ground the pattha-as-day-ration sense — the cross-reference the user intuited is in the commentary.
- SN 22.96 Mahāsudassana parallel uses the same *nāḷikodanaparamaṃ* metric as SN 3.13 — the canonical "moderate adult portion" is consistent across very different sutta-genres (king-cure vs. impermanence-meditation).

Cause: for open-ended "discover N good candidates across the whole canon" tasks, literary/pedagogical judgment benefits from the orchestrator doing the search itself rather than only delegating; Haiku sub-agents are reliable for citation existence but not always for content-summary accuracy under this kind of open brief. Cause: the orchestrator was not pinning `VICAYA_PHASE=2` on its own direct helper calls after handing Phase 2 to sub-agents, on the assumption the orchestrator's own calls were exempt (they are, per the skill, but only *before* any sub-agent gates the phase out from under it). Fix: once ANY sub-agent has been dispatched for a phase, the orchestrator itself should also pin `VICAYA_PHASE=<n>` on every direct helper call for that phase, not just sub-agents — the shared-pointer race applies to the orchestrator too, not only to sibling sub-agents. Scope: global (SKILL.md correction candidate).

Fix: always re-run `resolve-citation` on every distinct paranum before quoting it, even mid-flow within what looks like the same sutta's argument — subhead boundaries can appear without an obvious visual break. Scope: local (personal discipline reminder) / global (worth restating in Hard Rule 9's text explicitly for this exact pattern).

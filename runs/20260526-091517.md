## What surprised me

- The phrase *saha cittuppādāva* / *sahacittuppādā* appears ONLY in the two Devadatta passages (Cullavagga §333 and AN5.100) in the entire Pāḷi canon — no other puthujjana's iddhi loss is described this instantaneously. This is genuinely unique and not noted prominently in secondary literature.
- The canonical snake form is just *ahimekhalikā* (one snake as girdle); the seven-snake elaboration (2 on hands, 2 on legs, 1 neck, 1 head, 1 shoulder) is purely commentarial (Dhammapada-aṭṭhakathā) and has no canonical basis — a surprisingly large gap between canonical sparseness and commentarial dramatization.
- Lotus Sūtra Chapter 12 (Devadatta-parivarta) is near-universally considered a later addition by scholars, absent from some early manuscript traditions — the note now flags this clearly, which prior treatments often elide.

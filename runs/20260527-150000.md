## What surprised me
- The DN1 cosmogony (paras 40-41) where Brahma creates from past-life memory is more psychologically interesting than I expected — it's almost a phenomenological account of how a being generates the sense of a creator
- The Janavasabha Sutta (DN18) reads like an administrative report about non-human attendees at a monastic gathering — far more bureaucratic than mystical
- The Kevaddha Sutta's structure is devastating for the "deva-knowledge is important" position — even Brahmā doesn't know the answer

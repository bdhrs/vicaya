## What surprised me
- DPD's own data vindicates the correspondent: the "sudden" lemmas (āyataka 1.1, āyatakena ind) have zero attestation while the "long" lemma carries the only commentarial gloss — and DPD invented a phantom √yā homonym to host "sudden".
- The AN 5.209 aṭṭhakathā glosses the bare word directly as "āyatakenā'ti dīghena" — the single cleanest refutation of "suddenly", and it was sitting in the canon DB.
- The correspondent's argument is essentially Ṭhānissaro's published position; the one genuinely weak move is their own "after no long stretch (=quick)" reading, which inverts the anupubba thrust.

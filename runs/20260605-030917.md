## What surprised me
- Abhayagiri Vihāra is the critical bridge between Theravāda paritta and Mahāyāna dhāraṇī — a single Sri Lankan monastery (3rd–12th c.) was the transmission point where the two streams converged before diverging again after Parākramabāhu I's unification.
- The Burmese framing of paritta-as-external-protection + sati-as-internal-protection is a genuine doctrinal innovation that does not appear in Sri Lankan sources. Shwe Oo Min Sayadaw's formulation is the clearest statement.
- The Dipavamsa records the Buddha himself performing a protective "spell" (*rakkhā*) over Sri Lanka — a narrative that gives the entire island a mythic paritta origin story independent of the canonical texts.

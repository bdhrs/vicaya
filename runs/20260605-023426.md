## What surprised me
- The AN 5.52 "akusalarāsi" (mass of unwholesome) statement is remarkably strong — it says the hindrances ARE the mass of the unwholesome, not just a subset. This is more emphatic than commonly presented.
- The SN 46.52 Pariyāyasutta (5→10, 7→14 internal/external doubling) is a unique analytic move the Buddha claims as distinguishing his teaching from other ascetics — an underappreciated sutta.
- Calibre FTS returned rich canonical Pāḷi snippets (not just metadata) — the Paṭisambhidāmagga's expansion to include arati and "all akusala dhammā" as nīvaraṇas is significant for Section 2.

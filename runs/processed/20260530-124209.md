## What surprised me
- MN117 Mahācattārīsaka contains the full `takko vitakko saṅkappo appanā byappanā cetaso abhiniropanā vacīsaṅkhāro` synonym list at the sutta level — previously assumed Abhidhamma-only. This changes the historical argument: the appanā/abhiniropana vocabulary is canonical-sutta, not a post-canonical innovation.
- MN128 has the Buddha himself biographically listing all three samādhi types (savitakka-savicāra / avitakka-vicāramatta / avitakka-avicāra), so the three-concentration schema is not just a DN33/Netti doxography — the Buddha owns it.
- The Atthasālinī explicitly cites Nāgasena (Milinda) by name for the bell simile — a clear textual transmission chain that strengthens Cousins (1992)'s argument.

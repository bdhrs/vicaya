## What surprised me
- The three-lives interpretation (which dominates Western dharma books) is actually contested within Theravāda itself by reformist teachers like Ajahn Buddhadāsa
- The "interdependence" concept popular in contemporary Engaged Buddhism is a relatively modern development, not the original teaching
- Huayan/Flower Garland school has the most radical expansion of dependent origination into universal mutual interpenetration
- Madhyamaka interpretation equates paṭiccasamuppāda directly with śūnyatā - this is a philosophical leap not present in the suttas

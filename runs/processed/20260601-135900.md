## What surprised me
- The two canonical "cannot" lists (AN10.72 thorns, AN9.31 cessations) map almost one-to-one onto the 2024–25 7T-fMRI jhāna findings (reduced sounds, reduced narrative thought, volition lost at depth) — a clean textual/empirical convergence.
- MN111 (the strongest proof-text for fine-grained insight *within* jhāna) has no Āgama parallel at all — so the boldest "can do insight in absorption" claim is also the least early/pan-sectarian.
- AN9.36's "yāvatā saññāsamāpatti tāvatā aññāpaṭivedho" cuts both ways: it licenses within/based-on-jhāna insight for the lower attainments *and* mandates emerge-then-reflect for the top two.

## What I'd change in the skill
- DOCTRINAL TRAP worth a SKILL note: for any jhāna question, (a) separate the SUTTA reading from the ABHIDHAMMA/commentarial reading explicitly — it is the governing polarity in this vault; and (b) never read *kaṇṭaka* (AN10.72 thorn) as a cessation. A thorn is a present disturbance you encounter ("women are a thorn to the holy life" → the monk still sees women), so "sound is a thorn to the first jhāna" is evidence sound IS heard and disturbs, NOT that it is absent. The cessation list (AN9.31, niruddhā) is the separate, literal one; the suttas place rūpasaññā ceasing only ABOVE the 4th jhāna, so "five senses shut off in jhāna" is commentarial, not sutta. I conflated these in the first draft and the user (correctly) sent it back.

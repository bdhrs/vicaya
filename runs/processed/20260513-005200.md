## What surprised me

- The canon SQLite `paranum` fields are empty for most rows in `s0402m3_mul` — the sutta text required direct `sqlite3` query by row `id` range rather than by `paranum`. The `resolve-citation s0402m3_mul 170` still worked correctly.
- The Manorathapūraṇī commentary explicitly links *dhammuddhacca* to the *dasavipassanupakkilesa* — this is a highly specific, unambiguous interpretation that resolves the "what is dhamma-agitation?" question clearly, yet it is rarely foregrounded in English-language discussions.
- The Chinese parallel (MA 75) omits the fourth case entirely, which has major text-critical implications — Analayo's note on this is load-bearing for the debate about the fourth case's provenance.

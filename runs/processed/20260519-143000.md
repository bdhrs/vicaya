## What surprised me

- The question was confirmed strongly: the saw simile for ānāpānassati does not appear in MN 118, MN 10, DN 22, SN 54, or anywhere in the four main Nikāyas. Zero hits for `kakac` across all those books. The user's suspicion was correct.
- The Visuddhimagga explicitly cites the Paṭisambhidāmagga when introducing the saw simile — Buddhaghosa does not claim to author it, does not attribute it to the Buddha; he quotes it as "it was said" with a clear Paṭis. reference. The misattribution is entirely in popular transmission.
- The lathe-worker (*bhamakāra*) simile is the actual canonical ānāpānassati simile in DN22/MN10 — but it's not about a fixed contact point at all; it's about knowing long vs. short, which maps to the first two steps of the sixteen, not to the nose-tip-fixation pedagogy the saw simile supports.
- MN 118 (the ānāpānassati sutta par excellence) contains neither simile. Both the lathe-worker and the saw are absent from the sutta whose practice they supposedly illustrate.
- arrowriver.ca has a direct false attribution: "Buddha himself in DN 22." The search conclusively refutes this — zero `kakac` hits in all DN books.

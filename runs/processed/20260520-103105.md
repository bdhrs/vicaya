## What surprised me
- The same formula "yaṃ vedeti taṃ sañjānāti" bears two opposite readings in two different suttas (sequential in MN 18, simultaneity-proof in MN 43). The commentary on MN 43 with its ekuppāda-ekanirodha-ekavatthuka-ekārammaṇa quadrad is the cleanest formulation in the canon-commentary corpus for "what 'inseparable' actually means."
- MN 44's classification of saññā+vedanā as the cittasaṅkhāra is the direct sutta-level scriptural authority for the Abhidhamma cetasika pairing — I had not appreciated this connection before. The cross-check surfaced it indirectly via the pointer to citta-saṅkhāra.

## What surprised me
- The suttas themselves contain *both* cessationist and realist language - no clear resolution in the earliest layer
- The SN43 "unconditioned" definition is explicitly cessation of defilements, not a thing
- But DN16 treats Nibbāna-dhātu as a "place" one enters - same canon, contradictory frames
- The Thai Dhammakāya debate (1999) shows this isn't just an academic question - it's live in contemporary Buddhism

## What surprised me
- The strongest non-simile evidence is structural, not lexical: the anupubbanirodha series (AN9.31 / SN36.11) puts rūpasaññā ceasing only at infinite space, above the 4th jhāna — sidestepping the contested word kāya entirely.
- Anālayo (Plumbing the Limits) reads the *same* passages (sound-as-thorn, breath-cessation) for the *opposite* conclusion — making him the strongest opposing voice, not an ally of the embodied camp as I'd assumed.
- SN48.40 genuinely cuts against a naive "body fully felt till the 4th" — bodily feeling-faculties refine away by the 3rd; the honest reading distinguishes vedanā-faculties from rūpasaññā/breath-body.

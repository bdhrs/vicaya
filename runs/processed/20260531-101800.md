## What surprised me
- The MN43 "viññāṇa among the three that leave a corpse" detail is the precise textual hinge for the entire intra-Buddhist acittaka-vs-ālaya dispute — the sutta's silence on whether consciousness remains is what each school fills in differently. A rare case where a single canonical omission generates a whole comparative literature.
- The Madhyamāgama parallel MA210 gives a *causal* account of emergence ("caused by body and the six sense fields, conditioned by the life faculty") that the Pāḷi MN44 lacks — a concrete recension-level contradiction that maps directly onto the Sautrāntika/Yogācāra debate.
- The library held a monograph devoted solely to this attainment (Arbel #2655) plus Griffiths, Schmithausen, and a 2024 EEG paper — unusually deep coverage for one narrow term.

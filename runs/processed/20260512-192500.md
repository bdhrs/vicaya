## What surprised me
- AN 8.63 treats upekkha with the exact same jhana formula as metta -- no canonical warrant for the Vism's "single jhAna for upekkha" claim. The suttas give upekkha the full 7-variant samadhi treatment alongside metta, contradicting the dominant Visuddhimagga model that upekkha is only accessible after mastering the third jhana in the first three brahmaviharas.
- The Pa_tisambhida_magga is itself a canonical text (Khuddaka Nika_ya, 12th book), not a later commentary -- the systematization of metta into 528 pervasion modes is already in the Pali Canon, not invented by the Vism. The Vism inherited the systematization but added the temperament classification, the self-first progression, and the access/absorption framework.
- "Appama_na" (immeasurable) in the suttas is a quality-of-mind term (freedom from boundaries and resentment), not primarily a quantity-of-beings term. The shift to counting beings is visible even in the canonical Pa_tisambhida_magga, making it a gradual drift rather than a single sudden innovation.

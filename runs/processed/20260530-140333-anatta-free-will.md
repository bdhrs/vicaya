## Scope
Thematic/philosophical (not anchored to a single sutta). Maps five major positions: libertarian free will, hard determinism/fatalism, Theravāda compatibilist reading, Siderits's paleo-compatibilism, and the emancipatory reading. ## Key Findings
1. SN22.59 Anattalakkhaṇa argument (aggregates can't be commanded at will) directly refutes the ātman presupposed by libertarian free will.
2. AN3.62 Buddha explicitly rejects all three determinism variants (pubbekatahetu, issaranimmāna, ahetuappaccaya) as leading to moral paralysis.
3. AN6.63 "Cetanāhaṃ, bhikkhave, kammaṃ vadāmi" — the positive account: agency is cetanā, a conditioned mental factor, not a fixed self.
4. Siderits's paleo-compatibilism (ultimate no-agents; conventional trained-cetanā = weak free action) is the most precise modern reconstruction.
5. Adam (JIABS 2010): the Buddha gives a negative answer to metaphysical libertarian free will and a positive answer to moral responsibility — different questions.
6. Harvey/Bodhi: "free will vs determinism" is itself a category mistake — the self-presupposition generating the Western problem is rejected at the root.

## What surprised me

- The bee simile — the most famous Visuddhimagga simile for vitakka/vicāra — is Buddhaghosa's own invention, first introduced in the Samantapāsādikā (Vinaya commentary) and not found in any prior Pāḷi or Sanskrit source. Cousins (1992) is unambiguous on this.
- The bird simile does predate Buddhaghosa (Peṭakopadesa), but the bee simile doesn't. Most modern discussions treat both as equally ancient.
- The Yogasūtra's vitarka/vicāra framework (I.17, I.42–45) borrowed from Buddhism, not the reverse — and the direction of influence question had a cleaner answer than expected given the dating.
- C.A.F. Rhys Davids (1900) already used "initial application" for vitakka in her Dhammasaṅganī translation, anticipating the VM-aligned rendering by decades before it became standard PTS practice.
- Sujato is the only translator using context-sensitive different renderings for the same Pāḷi word (vitakka = "placing the mind" in jhāna; "thought" in ordinary discourse) — a principled response to the sutta-vs-VM tension that deserves to be highlighted explicitly.

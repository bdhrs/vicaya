## What surprised me
- The cross-check surfaced several genuinely useful corrections that I hadn't fully internalised — particularly that the Visuddhimagga *swaps* the kasiṇa list (drops viññāṇa+ākāsa, adds āloka+paricchinnākāsa) rather than simply preserving the canon list. Worth integrating verbatim.
- The terminological shift from *saññā* (perception to be developed) to *kammaṭṭhāna* (object of work) is a much larger evolutionary fact than I'd seen named in the prior literature — the cross-check helped me articulate it.
- The displacement of cemetery contemplations and the 32 parts from Western practice is essentially total, but Crosby's *borān kammaṭṭhāna* work shows that the *pre-modern* Theravāda mainstream had even *more* embodied/visualisation practice than the Visuddhimagga prescribes.

## What surprised me
- The word *apaṇṇaka* appears in the canon explicitly applied to the *rebirth outcome* (MN60 §103: "apaṇṇakaṃ me tatrūpapatti bhavissati"). Ṭhānissaro's "safe-bet" gloss isn't a modern conceit — it tracks the Buddha's own usage.
- MN 60 isn't only about rebirth — it's the same wager logic applied across five different metaphysical disputes. Easy to flatten this to "the rebirth argument" and miss the generality.

## What I'd change in the skill
- Add a note in the canon-search guidance that when a sutta runs an argument across multiple parallel paragraphs (like MN60's five pairs), the right move is to pull the *whole sequence* (e.g. paras 92–106) via the sqlite path, not to settle for whichever 2-3 hits the keyword search returns. The current SKILL.md doesn't explicitly cover this case.

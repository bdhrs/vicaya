## What surprised me
- The Papañcasūdanī commentary on MN 49 (s0201a_att) is unambiguous that *viññāṇaṃ anidassanaṃ* refers to Nibbāna, not to a transcendent consciousness. That's the cleanest single-source rebuttal to the Thanissaro/Forest reading and it lives right in the commentary table.
- SN 43's synonym list explicitly places *anidassana* in a chain of 33 Nibbāna-epithets. The user's reference list flagged this and the canon confirms it precisely.
- Library has both *Acariya Mun — A Spiritual Biography* and *Arahattamagga Arahattaphala* — i.e. exactly the two Maha Boowa texts the question rests on. Calibre metadata search was sufficient even without FTS.

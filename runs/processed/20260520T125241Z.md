# Run: monks-bowl-sizes — 2026-05-20

## Question
Monk bowl sizes: textual evidence (Vinaya/commentaries with Pāḷi terms and measurements), archaeological evidence, prominent archaeologists to contact, and hypothesis assessment (modern bowls much larger than canonical).

## What went well
- Found the canonical Vinaya passage (NP 21, vin01m_mul:602) immediately
- Found the Samantapāsādikā measurement passage (vin01a_att:602) with nāḷī = 13.5 palas
- Found MN 77:242 as the key "eating full bowl" sutta — the passage the user was paraphrasing
- Web sources gave modern bowl size data (Vinaya-mukha experiment, Thai bowl sizes) cleanly
- Scholars list assembled from WAC-10 session + web search

## What was harder
- The "two or three bowls" user clue required 5+ search variations to track down; the actual text says "samatittika or bhiyyo" (fill the bowl or more), not a specific "two or three"
- Magadhan nāḷī → litres conversion chain is approximate; pala is weight (not volume), requiring grain density assumptions
- Gemini cross-check timed out both attempts; crossed without it

## Key finding missed in a prior pass
- Nine-bowl sub-classification from Samantapāsādikā (compaction recovery confirmed this was found in Phase 2)

## Self-improvement note
- When searching for a user's remembered sutta passage, try "samatittika" (level with brim) as a Pāḷi term — it is the canonical term for "filling the bowl" and leads directly to the passage

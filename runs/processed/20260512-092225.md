## What surprised me
- The Paṭisambhidāmagga (PM) already introduces a proto-nimitta (*nāsikagge vā mukhanimitte vā*) for parimukhaṃ — making the PM a genuine intermediary, not just a list-preserving text.
- AN9.36 (Jhānasuttaṃ) is the clearest canonical statement that insight occurs *within* jhāna, yet it is almost never cited in the "insight before or after?" debate.
- The Peṭakopadesa's *ākotana/anumajjana* definitions for vitakka/vicāra are the "missing link" in the semantic shift from "thinking" to "attention-application."

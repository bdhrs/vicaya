## What surprised me
- The ariyasacca formula is remarkably stable across all four Nikāyas — nearly verbatim identical from DN22, MN10, MN141, SN56.11, and the Vibhaṅga
- The sutta mūla provides multiple etymologies for "ariya" (truths as "real/not false" AND as "discovered by the noble one") — commentary didn't invent these
- SN 56.27-28 are verified real suttas but were flagged [REJECTED] by cross-check — likely a dpd.db sutta_info gap for SN56 sub-suttas above 56.20

## What I'd change in the skill
- The cross-check rejection of SN56.27-28 despite verify-citation confirming them suggests the dpd.db sutta_info completeness check has a blind spot for the Saccasaṃyutta's higher-numbered suttas

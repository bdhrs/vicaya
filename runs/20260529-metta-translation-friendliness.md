## Key Finding
The Visuddhimagga (§263) explicitly names *sinehasambhavo* (arising of worldly affection/fondness) as mettā's *vipatti* (failure state). *Sinehā* is the exact semantic field occupied by "friendliness" in English. The canonical formulas (Paṭisambhidāmagga, DN13, AN passages) define mettā apophatically — by absence of ill-will — not by presence of social warmth. Thanissaro explicitly argues against "friendliness" as too social. Support for "friendliness" exists (etymological, used as one of several glosses) but no major modern authority defends it as the *primary* or *best* rendering.

## Evidence Tiers for Claim
- **T1 (mūla)**: Strong — all brahmavihārā formulas apophatic; AN passages define mettā functionally as byāpāda antidote
- **T2 (Visuddhimagga)**: Decisive — sinehā = vipatti
- **T3 (modern scholars/translators)**: Consistent — Thanissaro (explicit), Narada (goodwill/benevolence best), Gombrich (absence of hatred), Francis Story (agapē/benevolence)

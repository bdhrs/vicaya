## What surprised me
- The sharp sleep-vs-nirodha contrast the question presumes is *not* a sutta theme. The mūla suttas contrast cessation with **death** (MN43 corpse-comparison), never with sleep; the fine-grained sleep distinction is entirely supplied by the Milindapañha + Abhidhamma layer. Naming this tier-caveat up front became the spine of the whole note.
- The Milindapañha's Supinapañho three-stage anatomy of falling asleep (heaviness → kapiniddā "monkey-sleep" where dreams occur → bhavaṅga, dreamless) maps almost exactly onto the modern NREM/REM distinction — a genuinely striking convergence for a ~2nd-c. BCE text.
- The mirror simile appears independently in both the Milindapañha (body/torpor/citta) and Śaṅkara's Advaita (reflection returns when the internal organ ceases) — convergent phenomenology, opposite metaphysics (anatta vs witness-Self). That inversion is the deepest divergence and the most useful comparative payoff.

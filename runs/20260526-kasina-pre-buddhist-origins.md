**Key findings that surprised me:**
- The compound *kṛtsnāyatana* has an asterisk in the Princeton Dictionary — it's reconstructed from Pāḷi, not independently attested in pre-Buddhist Brahmanical Sanskrit. I had assumed the Sanskrit form existed pre-Buddhism.
- Only the 3rd and 4th arūpas (ākiñcaññāyatana, nevasaññānāsaññāyatana) are demonstrably pre-Buddhist; the 1st and 2nd are Buddhist systematization.
- MN49 Brahmanimantanikasuttaṃ contains a direct Buddhist *critique* of element-meditation as Brahmanical territory — Brahmā explicitly says adhering to earth/water/fire/air places one in his domain. This is the canonical Buddhist counterstatement to the Brahmanical use of element-meditation.
- The four colour kasinas have NO clear Sanskrit pre-Buddhist antecedent — they appear to be distinctly Buddhist.
- Cross-check suggested one nuance: EA 35.9 includes "light" instead of "space" as a kasina, so the Āgama confirmation is "broadly corresponding" rather than identical.

**Scholarly landscape:** Three-way tension between Wynne (Brahmanical continuity), Bronkhorst (Buddhist innovation / legendary account), and the shared-pool model. The evidence actually supports all three for different parts of the kasina system: the arūpas and ākāśa meditation vocabulary are Brahmanical; the ten-fold system is Buddhist; the four colours are distinctly Buddhist with no clear antecedent.

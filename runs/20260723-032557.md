# Run Reflection — buddha-flying-srilanka-burma

## Summary

Thematic reference survey across CST canon database, Calibre library, and web sources for iddhi references connected to the Buddha visiting Sri Lanka and Burma. Run spanned two context windows (context compacted at Phase 4c/Phase 5 boundary); resumed cleanly via `scratch-resume`.

## Key findings

1. **Textual tier mismatch in the original question**: The question asked for "commentaries and subcommentaries" but the primary narrative of the Buddha's three visits to Sri Lanka (Mahāvaṃsa Ch. 1) is in the vaṃsa chronicles, not the canonical aṭṭhakathā. The canonical Samantapāsādikā narrates Mahinda's iddhi flight and Soṇa/Uttara's iddhi at Suvannabhūmi — but not the Buddha's own three visits to Lanka.

2. **Canonical aṭṭhakathā iddhi content found (Samantapāsādikā, vin01a_att nidānakathā)**:
   - Mahinda: aerial flight from Veṭisaka mountain to Missaka/Cetiyapabbata ("attasattamo vehāsaṃ uppatitvā")
   - Soṇa/Uttara: bodily multiplication at Suvannabhūmi ("diguṇe attabhāve nimminitvā")
   - Buddha's fifth resolution at Parinibbāna: relics to perform Twin Miracle at Great Cetiya in Lanka ("vehāsaṃ abbhuggantvā yamakapāṭihāriyaṃ karontū")
   - Buddha's Bodhi tree prophecy about Lanka (buddhacakkhunā lokaṃ voloketvā)
   - Two Pācittiya-aṭṭhakathā passages (vin02a2/3_att) on Nāgadīpa cetiya and end-time relic gathering

3. **Burma — critical finding**: No canonical Pāḷi aṭṭhakathā narrates the Buddha personally visiting Burma. Only the Sāsanavaṃsa (1861 CE, Paññāsāmi) contains such accounts — a very late chronicle, not canonical commentary.

4. **Suvannabhūmi Vinaya reference**: vin02a1_att §189 treats Suvannabhūmi as a factual maritime destination (no Vinaya offense for sea travel there), confirming the canonical commentary's presupposition of Suvannabhūmi as a real location.

## Key findings

1. **Claim 1 (nirodha without anāgāmī/arahant):** Not a sutta contradiction; contradicts Visuddhimagga XXIII. No early sutta examined states the restriction; the co-occurrence formula in AN 9.45 is ambiguous about whether taint-destruction is prerequisite or co-incident with nirodha entry.

2. **Claim 2 (jhānas non-absorption, body perceived):** Well supported by suttas — all four similes in DN 2 explicitly pervade "this very body"; third jhāna formula says "experiences pleasure with the body."

3. **Claim 3 ("hold thoughts" for 2nd jhāna):** Pedagogical divergence (sutta uses vūpasamā = subsiding), not doctrinal contradiction.

4. **Claim 4 (long/short breath = depth):** No sutta evidence either way.

5. **Claim 5 (selflessness = Nibbāna):** Meaningful distortion — suttas define Nibbāna as destruction of three roots via Noble Eightfold Path (SN 38.1), not as a nondual experiential state.

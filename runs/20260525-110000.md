## What surprised me
- The Sāriputta reference the user remembered is *commentarial*, not mūla: per the Cariyāpiṭaka-aṭṭhakathā, the Buddha narrated the Cariyāpiṭaka at Sāriputta's request. There's *also* a mūla Apadāna verse predicting Sāriputta's paññā-pāramī, which sits at the transition between "pāramī as individual mastery" and "the ten-fold list."
- The Cariyāpiṭaka illustrates only 6 of the 10 pāramī (no stories for paññā/viriya/khanti). Norman flags this asymmetry; Appleton makes it diagnostic for the hypothesis that the ten-list was retrofitted onto a smaller original.
- Norman's explicit "Buddhavaṃsa was not accepted as canonical by the Dīgha-bhāṇakas" — a hard sectarian datum that turns the abstract "late stratum" into a concrete intra-Theravāda dispute over what counts as canon.
